Pyschiatry

  • What is Pyschiatry?
    •
    • Focused on the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of mental, emotional and behavioral disorders.
    • Psychiatrists are qualified to assess both the mental and physcial aspects of physchological problems.
    • Some reasons people seek Psychiatrists is; panic attacks, frightening hallucinations, thoughts of suicide, or hearing "voices."
    • Sone long term reasons would be having feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or anxiousness that never seem to lift or problems functioning.
      •
  • What do Psychiatrists do to diagnose?
    •
    • Psychiatrists can order or perform a full range of medical laboratory and psychological tests that include; discussions with patients, help provide a picture of a patient's physical and mental state.
    • Specific diagnoses are based on criteria established in APA's Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders(DSM-5), which contains descriptions, symptoms and other criteria for diagnosing mental disorders
    • Treatments: various forms of psychotherapy, medications, psychosocial interventions and other treatments depending on what each patient needs.
    • Psychotherapy: is a treatment that involves a talking relationship between a therapist and patient.
    • Most medications are used by Psychiatrists in much the same way that medications are used to treat high blood pressure or diabetes.
      •
  • Class of Medications:
    •
      1. Antidepressants- used to treat depression, panic disorder, PTSD, anxiety, obsessive-compulsive disorder, borderline personality disorder and eating disorders.
      2. Antipsychotic medications- used to treat psychotic symptoms (delusions and hallucinations), schizophrenia, bipolar disorder.
      3. Sedatives and anxiolytics- used to treat anxiety and insomnia.
      4. Hypnotics- used to induce and maintain sleep.
      5. Mood stabilizers- used to treat bipolar disorder.
      6. Stimulants- used to treat ADHD.
